Legacy

Pippen is renowned as one of the greatest defenders in basketball history and an exceptionally versatile and agile player overall. In 2022, on the occasion of the NBA's 75th Anniversary, The Athletic ranked the top 75 players of all time, placing Pippen as the 32nd greatest player in NBA history. Similar to his teammate Michael Jordan, Pippen exhibited tenacious on-the-ball perimeter defense and was also effective as a help defender. His exceptional athleticism, even by professional athletes' standards, coupled with his basketball skills, set him apart. With his long arms (featuring a 2.2-meter wingspan) and leaping ability, Pippen disrupted passing lanes, blocked shots from behind, secured difficult rebounds, executed impressive mid-air plays, and made passes around defenders that most players couldn't accomplish. He often led the Bulls in assists and blocks, showcasing his well-rounded game. Pippen was known for his selflessness on the court, and his team-oriented approach played a crucial role in the Bulls' championship victories. His career total of 6,135 assists (averaging 5.2 per game) at the time of his retirement ranked 23rd all-time among all players, exemplifying his team-first mentality. Pippen's intense work ethic and remarkable physical abilities allowed him to consistently make highlight-reel plays, such as applying defensive pressure, causing turnovers, stealing the ball, and initiating fast breaks that he punctuated with powerful slam dunks. He displayed his leaping prowess during the 1990 Slam Dunk Contest with a dunk from the free-throw line. Pippen was an impressive finisher at the rim, utilizing both emphatic dunks and a refined finger roll as part of his skill set. He was also a prolific perimeter shooter for his era, tallying around 3,000 attempts and nearly 1,000 made three-pointers in his career. Several NBA players, including Jordan, Jason Kidd, and Karl Malone, have included Pippen in their all-time starting lineups. To commemorate the 20th anniversary of their first NBA Championship in 1991, the Chicago Bulls organization held a ceremony during halftime of a game against the Utah Jazz on March 12, 2011, honoring the 1991 championship team. Pippen and Jordan were present, reuniting with former teammates and coaches. The Bulls organization announced on March 17, 2011, that Pippen would be honored with a bronze statue at the United Center, the team's home arena. During the unveiling on April 7, 2011, in a halftime ceremony of a game against the Boston Celtics, Pippen expressed his gratitude for the incredible honor bestowed upon him.
